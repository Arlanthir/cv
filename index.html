Bio

I'm a software engineer with a passion for all things digital. When my father handed me down his old computer (my first), I immediately knew what I wanted to do for a living. Since that moment, I've been pursuing my love for technology in many different ways. I wrote a multiplayer text-based RPG in the golden age of mIRC, a sleeping/feeding time diary for new moms wearing Pebble smartwatches, I released the first version of the Portuguese card game Sueca in Google Play Store, among others.

Nowadays, I'm playing my part in the adoption of what I believe is the technology of the future: Progressive Web Apps. Soon, install wizards will be a thing of the past as the web jumps out of website and into the operating system. Now is the time to leverage this collision of worlds and achieve true harmony in the application ecosystem.

Game Boy Zero in development

  • Raspberry Pi 3

Inspired by the multiple Game Boy Zero projects of the maker community, I decided to make my own. A Game Boy Zero is a homemade handheld gaming console that looks exactly like an old Game Boy but is capable of emulating many retro gaming systems by using a Raspberry Pi Zero (hence the name). My version of the console currently uses a Raspberry Pi 3, which is harder to fit inside the Game Boy shell but provides better performance.
